(Sacramento) -
Assemblymember Mike A. Gipson (D-Carson) was appointed to the
Council of State Government (CSG) by the Speaker of the Assembly Toni Atkins. Gipson will sit on
the Finance Policy Committee, which gives members an opportunity to discuss issues relating to
state budgets and fiscal trends.
"It is an honor to have been appointed to the Finance Committee of the Council of State
Government," states Gipson. "As our core responsibilities include discussing the continued impact
of recessions, taxation, and funding social programs, I am excited to participate in crafting
policy that will help build a more equitable and stable economic foundation for generations to
come."
CSG is comprised of state legislators from around the nation organized into ten policy
committees with the purpose to promote cooperation and identify solutions to address issues of
common concern.
"A common problem among states has been the uneven economic recovery following the recent
recession," states Gipson. "In California, although we have seen a steady growth in our
productivity and a drop in unemployment, many families are still having trouble making ends meet.
As a member of the CSG Finance Committee, I hope to engage other lawmakers in discussions focusing
on expanding our economic prosperity to include those at lower end of the socio-economic
scale."
Mike Gipson represents the 64th Assembly District. The district includes the cities of
Carson, Compton, Gardena, Harbor Gateway, Lynwood, North Long Beach, Rancho Dominguez, South Los
Angeles, Torrance, Watts/Willowbrook and Wilmington.
