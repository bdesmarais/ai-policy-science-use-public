Gipson Co-Authors House Resolution to
Recognize and Celebrate Cinco de Mayo
(SACRAMENTO, Calif) – Assemblymember Mike A. Gipson, Chair of the Democratic Caucus, co-authored House Resolution (HR) 17 today to declare May 1-5 Cinco de Mayo week and honor the valiant spirit of the brave Mexicans who defeated the French army in the town of Puebla.
“As we celebrate this historical and cultural week in our history, we must continue with the same pride, dignity, courage, and respect that must be given not only to us here in America, but to those in Mexico who fought to seek justice and liberation” said Asm. Gipson. “As Democratic Caucus Chair, there is no better way to acknowledge the contributions made by our Latino community than to continue to protect and promote good immigration policies that represent the diversities of our golden state.”
Each Cinco de Mayo, the state legislature honors the valor of the outgunned Mexican militia in their improbable victory over the Imperial French army at the Battle of Puebla, 155 years ago. May 5 serves as an opportunity to celebrate the generations of contributions that Mexican Americans and all Latino Americans have made to the United States.

Resolución de cámara de autores GIPSON a reconocer y celebrar Cinco de Mayo
(SACRAMENTO, Calif) – Asambleísta Mike A. Gipson, Presidenta de la bancada democrática, coautor de casa resolución (HR) 17 hoy para declarar 1-5 de mayo Cinco de Mayo semana y honrar el espíritu valiente de los valientes mexicanos que derrotaron al ejército francés en la ciudad de Puebla.
"Celebramos esta semana histórica y cultural de nuestra historia, tenemos que seguir con el mismo orgullo, dignidad, valor y respeto que debe darse no sólo a nosotros aquí en América, sino a los de México que lucharon por buscar justicia y liberación", dijo Asm. GIPSON. "Como silla del Caucus democrático, allí es no hay mejor manera de reconocer los aportes de nuestra comunidad Latina que sigue para proteger y promover las políticas de inmigración buena que representan la diversidad de nuestro estado dorado".
Each Cinco de Mayo, the state legislature honors the valor of the outgunned Mexican militia in their improbable victory over the Imperial French army at the Battle of Puebla, 155 years ago. May 5 serves as an opportunity to celebrate the generations of contributions that Mexican Americans and all Latino Americans have made to the United States.
