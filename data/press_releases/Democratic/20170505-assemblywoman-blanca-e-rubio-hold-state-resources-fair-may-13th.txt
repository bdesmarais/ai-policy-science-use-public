(West Covina)
– Assemblywoman Blanca E. Rubio (D-Baldwin Park) is proud to work with various state agencies to bring essential services right to her community. Constituents can speak to representatives who will be on hand to answer questions, provide helpful services, and assist with concerns.
“In the age of the internet it has become increasingly important to be able to speak to a real person, especially when dealing with bureaucracy,” said Rubio. “Inviting government agencies to our communities to provide the services we need in real-time is one way to help cut through the red tape.”
The State Resources Fair will be held from 9 a.m. – 1 p.m. in the Arts and Recreation Center located at 14403- E. Pacific Avenue in Baldwin Park. Assemblywoman Rubio’s staff will also be in attendance to offer weekend office hours to residents of the 48
th
Assembly District. For additional information regarding the State Resources Fair, please contact the District Office at (626) 960-4457.
The 48th Assembly District, is comprised of the cities of Azusa, Baldwin Park, Bradbury, City of Industry, Covina, Duarte, El Monte, Glendora, Irwindale, Monrovia, West Covina, and the San Gabriel Valley unincorporated areas of Los Angeles County, including Bassett, Charter Oak, Citrus, East Arcadia, Ramona, Valinda and West La Puente.
