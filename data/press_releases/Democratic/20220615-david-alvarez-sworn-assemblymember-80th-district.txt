(SACRAMENTO, CA) – David Alvarez was sworn in today as the Assemblymember for the 80th Assembly District, representing southern San Diego County.
“I am profoundly grateful for the trust placed in me by the voters of the 80th Assembly District,” said Assemblymember Alvarez. “Now is the time to get to work to address homelessness, reprioritize our children’s education, and make California an affordable place to live for working people and families.”
Assemblymember Alvarez is a small business owner and has served as a member of major San Diego public organizations including the San Diego County Regional Airport Authority, San Diego Association of Governments, and Metropolitan Transit System. He also served on the board of the California League of Cities’ Latino Caucus and the national board of Local Progress, a nonprofit dedicated to advancing a racial and economic justice agenda through all levels of local government.
A proud advocate of the underserved in San Diego, Assemblymember Alvarez has made economic and environmental justice for all communities one of his top priorities in office.
The district includes the San Diego Communities of Barrio Logan, Logan Heights, Sherman Heights, City Heights, Azalea Park, Otay Mesa and San Ysidro, as well as the cities of Chula Vista, National City and Bonita.
