SACRAMENTO, CA - Today,
Assemblywoman Sharon Quirk-Silva
(D-Fullerton) celebrated the successful passage of her Assembly Bills during the Assembly Suspense Hearing at the State Capitol. Covering a range of important topics such as Affordable Housing and Education, these bills underscore Quirk-Silva's unwavering commitment to advocating for the pressing needs of her district.
“I am deeply grateful and honored by the passage of my bills, as they allow me to continue serving the needs of the 67th District as their State Assemblywoman,” said Assemblywoman Quirk-Silva. “I extend my heartfelt thanks to my constituents for their trust and support. Through our legislation, we will continue to bring California's resources to our community.”
In a display of her legislative experience, Assemblywoman Quirk-Silva successfully shepherded seven bills through the Assembly Appropriations Committee Suspense Hearing. This achievement stands as a testament to her bold and innovative approach in presenting new ideas to the Assembly.
"Our legislation has been meticulously crafted to address the pressing needs of our district," emphasized Assemblywoman Quirk-Silva. "By tackling issues such as homelessness, affordable housing, and accessible higher education, we aim to bring about transformative change in our communities. I remain dedicated to prioritizing public safety, improving access to mental and behavioral healthcare, and supporting our small businesses. I will tirelessly fight for our communities and hold the state accountable for the people of Orange County."
Looking ahead, Assemblywoman Quirk-Silva eagerly anticipates the further advancement of her bills through the Assembly floor, with the ultimate goal of garnering support in the Senate. As the legislative session continues, she remains resolute in her commitment to driving meaningful change and securing a brighter future for the constituents she serves.
Assemblywoman Sharon Quirk-Silva represents the 67th Assembly District, which includes the Orange County communities of Anaheim, Artesia, Buena Park, Cerritos, Cypress, Fullerton, Hawaiian Gardens, and La Palma.
