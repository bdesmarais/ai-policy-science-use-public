(SACRAMENTO, CA) – Assemblymember Steve Bennett (D-Ventura) honors Camarillo-based nonprofit Housing Trust Fund Ventura County as the 2024 California Nonprofit of the Year for Assembly District 38 (AD-38).
This is the ninth year of the California Nonprofit of the Year initiative, a state-wide effort honoring the critical role nonprofit organizations play in California.
"How we handle our housing crisis now dictates the future of our communities," said Assemblymember Bennett. "That is why the Housing Trust Fund's efforts to expand affordable and workforce housing plays a key role in addressing our crisis, and I am proud to uplift their work."
"We are honored to be recognized as Nonprofit of the Year 2024 by Assemblymember Bennett. This award reflects the tireless efforts of our team and our community of supporters who are dedicated to making affordable housing a reality because we believe that everyone deserves a home. This recognition strengthens our commitment to building stronger, more equitable communities, and we look forward to continuing our impactful work," said Linda Braunschweiger, CEO of the Housing Trust Fund Ventura County.
Housing Trust Fund VC joined fellow selected nonprofits in a celebratory luncheon in Sacramento on June 5, 2024.
Steve Bennett represents the 38th Assembly District, which includes Camarillo, Fillmore, Ojai, Oxnard, Port Hueneme, Santa Paula, and Ventura. He is chair of the Budget Subcommittee No. 4 on Climate Change, Natural Resources, and Transportation.
