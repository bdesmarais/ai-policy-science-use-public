SACRAMENTO, CA —
Today, the following seven (7) bills by Assemblymember Jesse Gabriel passed out of the California Senate Appropriations Committee:
AB 1053
-
Affordable Housing Construction Loans
Would reduce the cost of affordable housing by allowing, for the first time ever, developers to access construction funds during the building process.
AB 2316
-
School Food Safety Act
Would protect California students by prohibiting schools from serving foods that contain harmful chemicals linked to behavioral and developmental issues in children.
AB 2331
-
Voluntary Carbon Offset Transparency
Combats corporate greenwashing and protects the environment by improving transparency for voluntary carbon offset credits.
AB 2455
-
The Whistleblower Protection Enhancement Act
Strengthens and modernizes local and state whistleblower hotline laws to prevent the misuse of state funds and resources.
AB 2621
-
Hate Crime Reduction and Community Safety Act
Requires law enforcement training on hate crimes to include education on how gun violence restraining orders can prevent an escalation in hate-based violence.
AB 2899
-
Safe Nurse-to-Patient Ratios
Strengthens enforcement measures to ensure hospitals comply with safe nurse-to-patient staffing and prioritize patient safety.
AB 3160
-
Low-Income Housing Tax Credits
Secures $500 million annually in funding for Low-Income Housing Tax Credits through 2031, facilitating the construction of tens of thousands of affordable units across California.
Following passage from the Senate Appropriations Committee, these bills now advance to the Senate Floor for a vote.
