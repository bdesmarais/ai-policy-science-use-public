Sacramento, CA –
Assemblymember Juan Carrillo is thrilled to announce that Governor Gavin Newsom has officially signed ten of his proposed bills into law. This landmark achievement underscores the Assemblymember's commitment to addressing critical issues affecting our desert communities and enhancing the lives of all constituents.
The newly enacted legislation covers a range of important topics, including:
AB 2082 – Relinquishment of Palmdale Boulevard
AB 2109 – Industrial Process Heat Recovery
AB 2192 – California Uniform Construction Cost Accounting Act
AB 2245 – Retaining Career Technical Education Teachers
AB 2371 – Electric Security Fence Permits
AB 2443 – Transactions & Use Tax
AB 2533 – Accessory Dwelling Unit Amnesty
AB 2622 – Expanding Opportunities for Handymen
AB 2929 – Advancing Foster Youth Family Finding Efforts
AB 98 – Planning Logistics & Neighborhood Standards (P.L.A.N.S) Act
“I am incredibly proud to see these vital pieces of legislation signed into law,” said Assemblymember Carrillo. “Each bill represents the voices and needs of our communities, addressing the concerns that matter most to our residents. I want to thank my colleagues, staff, and the community members who have supported or strengthened these efforts.”
The Assemblymember emphasized the importance of collaboration and community advocacy in driving meaningful change. “Many of the bills presented in this session’s legislative package were requested or directly inspired by constituents, and community feedback played a crucial role in the amendments process. These laws are a testament to what we can accomplish when we work together. I am committed to continuing this momentum and ensuring that our community's voice is heard in Sacramento.”
As the Assemblymember prepares for the next legislative session, he remains dedicated to championing policies that promote local jobs, affordable housing, and accessible education opportunities, and looks forward to continuing to build on the success of his first term in the California Assembly.
