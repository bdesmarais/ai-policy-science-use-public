Sacramento, CA
– Assemblywoman Cottie Petrie-Norris was sworn in for fourth new term representing the cities of Irvine, Costa Mesa and Tustin in the 73rd Assembly District.
“It is an honor and privilege to continue serving the residents of California’s 73rd Assembly District,” said Assemblywoman Petrie-Norris (D-Irvine).
“
I am deeply grateful for the trust my constituents have placed in me. Together, we will advance policies that protect our environment, build a reliable and affordable green economy, strengthen public safety, and deliver tangible results that make a real difference for our communities and our state.”
In the 2025 legislative session, Assemblywoman Petrie-Norris is focused on addressing key priorities:
Combatting Climate Change: Lowering energy costs for consumers and accelerating California’s transition to a sustainable, green economy.
Strengthening the Economy: Supporting small businesses, fostering innovation, and enhancing California’s global competitiveness.
Enhancing Public Safety: Advancing policies to reduce crime, support first responders, and keep our neighborhoods safe.
Expanding Access to Healthcare: Protecting reproductive rights and ensuring access to services for all Californians.
Assemblywoman Petrie-Norris is steadfast in her commitment to reflecting the values and aspirations of her constituents, working tirelessly to create better opportunities and improve the quality of life for families, workers, and businesses in the 73rd Assembly District.
