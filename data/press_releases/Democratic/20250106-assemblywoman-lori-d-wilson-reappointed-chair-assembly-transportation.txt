SACRAMENTO, CA
– Assemblywoman Lori D. Wilson (D-Suisun City) has been reappointed as Chair of the Assembly Transportation Committee for the 2025-2026 legislative session. This reappointment reaffirms her leadership in shaping California’s transportation policies to address the state’s growing needs and challenges.
“I am deeply honored to be reappointed as Chair of the Assembly Transportation Committee,” said Assemblywoman Wilson. “Thank you to Speaker Robert Rivas for entrusting me with this critical responsibility to guide California’s transportation policies. Transportation impacts every Californian—connecting people to opportunities, goods, and essential services while shaping our environment and economy. I am committed to ensuring our transportation system improves quality of life for all residents by fostering innovation, equity, and sustainability.”
As the first Black woman to chair the Assembly Transportation Committee, Assemblywoman Wilson has prioritized strengthening critical infrastructure, creating a forward-thinking, inclusive transportation framework that addresses climate change, enhances public transit, and promotes safety and accessibility for all Californians. Under her leadership, the committee will continue to advance policies that benefit both local communities and the state’s economic future.
Assemblywoman Wilson is excited to build on her work in collaboration with stakeholders and communities to ensure California’s transportation system meets the needs of today while preparing for the challenges of tomorrow.
