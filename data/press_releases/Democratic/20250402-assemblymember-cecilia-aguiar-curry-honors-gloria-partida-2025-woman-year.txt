(SACRAMENTO, CA)
— Assemblymember Cecilia Aguiar-Curry proudly celebrates Gloria Partida as Assembly District Four’s 2025 Woman of the Year, in recognition of her outstanding contributions to the community.
"Gloria's leadership and heartfelt dedication to uplifting others is what public service and this award are all about," said
Assemblymember Cecilia Aguiar-Curry
. "Her advocacy makes Davis a stronger, better community, and has been an inspiring example for the entire region. I am honored to recognize her as our 2025 Woman of the Year. We need more Glorias in this world.”
Each year, the California Women’s Legislative Caucus honors women from across the state who demonstrate exceptional service, leadership, and commitment to their communities. In Assembly District Four, Gloria Partida is making a profound impact through her decades-long work as a dedicated advocate, public servant, and civic leader. As the former mayor of Davis, current Davis city councilmember, chair of the Yolo-Solano Air Quality Management District, and co-founder and executive director of the Davis Phoenix Coalition, she has devoted her career and life to fostering social justice, disability rights, affordable housing, public safety, environmental sustainability, and community empowerment across Davis and Yolo County.
In receiving the award,
Gloria Partida
expressed her gratitude, "I’m deeply honored to be recognized by Assemblymember Aguiar-Curry. This award reflects the collective efforts of so many in our community who work every day to create a more just and compassionate world, and I’m proud to stand alongside them in this mission."
###
Assemblymember Cecilia Aguiar-Curry
represents California’s 4th Assembly District, which includes all of Lake, Colusa, Napa, and Yolo Counties, and part of Sonoma County. She serves as the Assembly Majority Leader and Chair of the California Women’s Legislative Caucus.
