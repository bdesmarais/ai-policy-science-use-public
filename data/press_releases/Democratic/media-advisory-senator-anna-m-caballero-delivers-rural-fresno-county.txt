Media Advisory Senator Anna M. Caballero Delivers for Rural Fresno County

SACRAMENTO: Senator Anna M. Caballero (D-Merced) successfully secured $10 million to fund essential infrastructure needs in the rural, agricultural west side areas of Fresno County. She will spend the day in each city receiving funding to tour the future facility sites.

The Senator’s Itinerary is as Follows:

Firebaugh: Friday, February 25th 10:00-11:00 AM $2 Million Public Safety Infrastructure

Who: •    Senator Anna Caballero •    Supervisor Pacheco •    Mayor Jenkins •    Councilmember Valdez •    Police Chief Raygoza •    City Manager Ben Gallegos

Location: Firebaugh Courthouse 1325 O Street Firebaugh Ca, 93622

San Joaquin: Friday, February 25th 12:00-1:00 PM $2 Million for Youth Programming

Who: •    Senator Caballero •    Mayor Hernandez •    Golden Plains Board President Parra •    Lt. Commander Brent Stalker •    City Manager Elizabeth Nunez

Location: Leo Cantu Community Center 22058 Railroad St. San Joaquin Ca, 93660

Huron: Friday, February 25th 3:00-4:00 PM $4 Million for Ambulance Bay

Who: •    Senator Anna Caballero •    Assemblymember Dr. Joaquin Arambula •    Mayor Leon •    Police Chief Turegano

Location: Huron Council Chambers 17051 12th St. Huron Ca, 93234

*The City of Mendota has previously received their award.

For interviews or inquiries, please contact Elisa Rivera at (209) 726-5495. Senator Anna M. Caballero represents the 12th Senate District, composed of the Salinas Valley, Merced and San Benito Counties, and portions of Fresno, Madera and Stanislaus Counties. ###
