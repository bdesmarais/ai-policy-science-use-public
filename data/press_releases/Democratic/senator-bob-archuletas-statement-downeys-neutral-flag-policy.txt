NORWALK, CA – The following is a statement from Senator Bob Archuleta regarding the Downey City Council’s passage of a neutral flag policy concerning the types of flags allowed to be flown on city property.

I know the members of the City Council, and have worked with them, but I don’t agree with this decision. I feel policy that affects the community should always be fair, equitable and inclusive of everyone.

Having said that, I read the new policy, and it is clear that the City Council still has both the opportunity and the time to reconsider their decision.  And, I am very hopeful that they do just that. We as a community should afford them the opportunity to do so.

I also empathize with our LGBTQ+ neighbors who may feel the new policy is aimed at them. I encourage them to read the new protocol and engage in conversation with their elected representatives on the city council.

Acknowledging Pride Month in June should not be controversial.

Contributions to the 30 th Senate District, the State of California and the Nation, come from all walks of life.

Let’s come together and give everyone an equal voice.

Senator Bob Archuleta currently represents the 30th Senate District, which includes portions of Los Angeles County and Orange County. He is the Chair of the Senate Committee on Military and Veterans Affairs, and a member of the Business, Professions and Economic Development Committee, Government Organization Committee, Transportation Committee, as well as the Joint Legislative Committee on Emergency Management.
