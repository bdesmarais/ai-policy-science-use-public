AJR 6 Urges U.S. Congress, President to Pause AI Development to Protect Humanity

SACRAMENTO, Calif., May 5, 2023
– Today
Assemblyman Bill Essayli
announced the introduction of his
Assembly Joint Resolution 6
, which urges the United States government to immediately pause the development of artificial intelligence systems for at least six months to develop safeguards for humanity.
“The future of humanity and how we live, work, and play, depends on thoughtful planning and management of the most advanced artificial intelligence systems. That is why I authored AJR 6 which calls on the United States government to immediately pause AI development for at least 6 months to allow time to develop necessary safeguards,” said
Assemblyman Essayli
“The rapid pace at which advanced AI systems like GPT-4 are developing is alarming and unprecedented. We must proceed with caution and consult with experts in the tech industry who are sounding the alarm about this technology,” added
Assemblyman Essayli
In March,
Elon Musk
joined the biggest names in tech
urging the world’s leading artificial intelligence labs to pause
the training of the new super-powerful systems for six months, saying that recent advances in AI present “profound risks to society and humanity.”
AJR 6
now awaits referral by the Assembly Rules Committee and may receive its first policy committee hearing in the coming weeks.
Assemblyman Bill Essayli represents the 63rd Assembly District in the California Legislature, which includes the whole cities of Norco, Menifee, Lake Elsinore, and Canyon Lake, as well as portions of the cities of Eastvale, Riverside, and Corona.
