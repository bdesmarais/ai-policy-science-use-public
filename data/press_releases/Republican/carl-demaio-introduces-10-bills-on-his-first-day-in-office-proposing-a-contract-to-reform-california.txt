Taking a page from the successful “Contract with America” thirty years ago, newly-elected California State Assemblymember Carl DeMaio has introduced 10 ambitious and sweeping bills on his first day in office that he dubs a “Contract to Reform California” to fix the state’s most pressing problems.
“Californians are fleeing our state in record numbers — and that’s why I’m calling on state leaders to act on these 10 common-sense proposals to give residents relief on their most pressing challenges before it is too late,” DeMaio says.
DeMaio points to the landslide victory
for
Prop 36 that shows that there is more agreement on common-sense reforms amongst average voters than is currently reflected in the divisive and partisan culture in the state legislature.
“It is my sincere hope that my colleagues — from both political parties — will join me in a creative and open-minded discussion on these and other solutions to these ten critical issues,” DeMaio says.
CARL DEMAIO’S FIRST 10 BILLS
THE CONTRACT TO REFORM CALIFORNIA
Cost-of-Living Reduction Act
DeMaio says California’s cost-of-living crisis was caused by insane regulations and high taxes. This law would provide each middle-class family with $2,500 in cost-of-living relief rebates annually — as well as suspend all state taxes and fees on gas and utilities until politicians reform state policies that spike costs. Once this model is proven successful in driving down energy and utility costs, it would be expanded to reduce costs in home insurance, housing, and healthcare. “If we want politicians to address the cost crisis, we have to
for
ce them to pay the price — not our families,” DeMaio declares.
Taxpayer Protection Act
This measure limits the ability of politicians to raise taxes — and prohibits them from imposing a new Mileage Tax, Exit Tax, or Wealth Tax — or increasing Gas or Car Taxes. The measure also restores a two-thirds vote requirement on local special tax increases and requires honest ballot titles with a “Tax Increase” warning be placed on any ballot measure that increases taxes.
Balanced Budget Accountability Act
“We can and must balance the budget without tax hikes and cuts in important services,” DeMaio pledges. To achieve that goal, this measure would lead to a responsible consensus by requiring a two-thirds vote of both houses of the Legislature to pass the budget — or impose or increase taxes or fees. The measure would also reduce expenses by cutting then capping total annual labor costs and imposing competitive sourcing reforms on all state agencies.
Education Choice and Parental Empowerment Act
“The only way to truly empower parents is to give them financial freedom to choose the best school
for
their child, “DeMaio says. That’s why DeMaio proposes this law to provide $13,000 annually in education vouchers
for
every student — starting with middle-class and low-income families and then expanding the program.
Homelessness Reduction Act
This law would end the corrupt and wasteful “Housing First” homeless model currently used and replace it with a “People First” model, which will redirect funds to programs that require mental health and substance abuse treatment to address the root causes of homelessness. The law would also prioritize expansion of shelter beds over permanent supportive housing, impose work requirements on individuals receiving assistance, and require the removal of homeless camps near schools and in public areas. Developers that give campaign contributions would be barred from receiving funds.
End Reckless
Release
of Criminals Act
“Passage of Prop 36 was just the first step in combatting the rising crime wave in California — now we must also end “reckless
release
” of criminals across our state,” DeMaio explains. This law would prohibit the early
release
of violent criminal and repeat offenders by repealing provisions of Prop 57 and prohibiting the placement of Sexually Violent Predators (SVPs) in residential communities. The law would also require mental health evaluations and referrals to proper treatment in any
release
decisions.
California Secure Borders Act
“Securing our border is crucial to protecting taxpayers, safeguarding communities, and ending the evils of human trafficking,” DeMaio explains. This law combats illegal immigration and helps secure the border by repealing the Sanctuary State law (SB 54 -2017) and ending taxpayer-funded handouts to illegal immigrants
for
welfare, healthcare, housing, travel, and other services. The law also provides
for
cross-deputization training of local law enforcement to support federal border security actions and provides standards
for
deployment of the national guard to the border.
Voter ID and Election Integrity Act
“The biggest threat to democracy is the loss of confidence in the integrity of our elections,” DeMaio says. Starting with the 2026 election, this law requires a government-issued ID to vote, strengthens citizenship verification of voter registrations, requires audits of voter lists to ensure they are properly maintained, imposes penalties on counties that fail to count ballots on time, and enhances signature reviews on ballots.
Punish Unconstitutional Actions Act
“California politicians have developed a bad habit of repeatedly imposing laws and orders that are blatantly unconstitutional — just to score political points while depriving citizens of their rights and
for
cing them to bear the upfront legal costs to regain them,” DeMaio says. This law would penalize the Governor who signs and any legislator who votes
for
a law that is later found to be unconstitutional in federal courts with a 25% cut in their annual state compensation
for
each offense.
Cut the Politicians’ Perks Act
“California politicians have granted themselves lavish perks and exemptions from state laws they impose on regular citizens,” DeMaio laments. This law would ban politicians from accepting gifts or trading in individual stocks; impose a lifetime lobbying ban; eliminate exemptions
for
the Legislature from labor, workplace, and public record laws; and eliminate pensions
for
local elected officials.
