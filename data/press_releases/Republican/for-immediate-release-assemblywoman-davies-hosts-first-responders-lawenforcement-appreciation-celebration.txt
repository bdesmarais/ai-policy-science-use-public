Laguna Niguel, CA – Today, Assemblywoman Laurie Davies hosted a ceremony to honor the first responders who tirelessly work to keep the community safe. Guests honored at the event included 911 dispatchers, law enforcement agencies, fire departments, crisis response organizations and residents from Laguna Niguel, San Clemente, San Juan Capistrano and Dana Point. “Bravery is what distinguishes first responders from the rest of us. As we leave danger, they run towards it. When we retreat, they stand on the front lines. It’s important to honor and thank the men and women who put it all on the line and protect our communities,” said Assemblywoman Davies (R- Orange County). First responders make sacrifices on behalf of the community every day without hesitation. Whether it’s responding to accidents and crime, a blazing fire, or traffic emergency, their service does not go unnoticed. Guests honored at the event included: 911 – OC Sheriff’s 911 Dispatch – Orange County Communications Crisis Response – Orange County Crisis Assessment Team (CAT) Law Enforcement – OC Sheriff’s Department – California Highway Patrol – OC Harbor Patrol – California State Park Rangers – Orange County Park Rangers Fire – OC Fire Authority (OCFA) Lifeguard – San Clemente Lifeguard Program – OC Lifeguards – California State Lifeguards Rank and File Associations – California Association of Highway Patrolmen – Association of California Sheriffs – Association of Orange County Deputy Sheriffs – OC Professional Firefighters Association Volunteer – Orange County’s Community Emergency Response Teams: o Laguna Niguel o San Clemente o Dana Point o San Juan Capistrano
