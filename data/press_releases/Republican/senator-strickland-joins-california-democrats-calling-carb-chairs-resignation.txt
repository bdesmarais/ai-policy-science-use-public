Senator Tony Strickland ( R-Huntington Beach ) joined several California Democrats in calling for the resignation of Liane Randolph, Chair of the California Air Resources Board (CARB).

Senator Strickland released the following statement:

“Finally, there is some common sense in California. CARB wields excessive power, yet these unelected bureaucrats lack accountability. Their pursuit of lofty climate change policies, without considering the true financial impact on the pocketbooks of California families, is unreasonable and out of touch. It is a good first step for the chair to resign.”

With two California oil refineries set to close, a report by USC Associate Professor Michael Mische has warned of an impending gas crisis this summer. The report predicts that drivers could be paying more than $8 per gallon for unleaded gas by 2026 due to a decrease in supplies.
