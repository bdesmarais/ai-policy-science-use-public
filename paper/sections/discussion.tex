\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{A competent judge is necessary, but not sufficient.} The most actionable lesson is
negative: our off-the-shelf zero-shot NLI configuration is not a safe stand-alone measure of whether
science supports a policy claim. It labels genuine support ``neutral'' because abstracts seldom
restate a press-release claim in entailing language, and the downward bias is large enough to flip a
substantive conclusion. But the corollary is \emph{not} ``therefore trust the generative judge.'' A
competent judge appears necessary, yet it is not sufficient: to license replacing human coders it must
itself be validated against a human standard \citep{calderon2025alttest}, which we have not done. We
present the NLI failure as a warning about a popular shortcut, not as proof that the generative judge
is right. (Strong claim verification, e.g.\ on SciFact \citep{wadden2020fact}, fine-tunes and selects
rationale sentences; our zero-shot setup is not a fair representative of that line of work.)

\paragraph{The part we did \emph{not} solve: validity without humans.} PPI \citep{angelopoulos2023ppi}
and DSL \citep{egami2023dsl} let researchers combine abundant imperfect labels with a small set of
\emph{trusted} labels. The tempting extension---that the trusted labels can come from an LLM rather
than a human---is exactly what we cannot justify: both methods, and the alternative-annotator test, are
defined relative to human ground truth, so substituting an unvalidated model for the human anchor
removes the guarantee rather than preserving it. Our intervals are therefore conditional on the judge.
We demonstrate the \emph{mechanics} of an autonomous surrogate-plus-judge-plus-PPI pipeline; we do not
demonstrate that its output is valid.

\paragraph{The all-LLM loop and self-preference (a first-order threat).} The deepest problem is
circularity. Our claims and references are LLM-produced and our judge is an LLM, and LLM evaluators are
documented to recognize and favor model-generated, low-perplexity text \citep{panickssery2024llm}; more
broadly, fluent model output should not be mistaken for knowledge \citep{bender2021stochastic}. A judge
that rates LLM-surfaced evidence as ``supportive'' may be exhibiting self-preference rather than
assessing science, and the $\sim$3$\times$ naive-to-PPI jump is the magnitude such a bias could produce.
Without a non-self anchor we cannot separate ``the judge corrects the surrogate'' from ``the judge
agrees with other models.'' This threatens the central estimate directly; it is not a footnote.

\paragraph{Diagnose across methods, not within---but a flag is not a validation.} One genuine lesson
survives: within-method agreement can be an illusion of correctness (our two NLI models agreed at
$\kappa=0.66$ while sharing a bias), so cross-method agreement is a more informative diagnostic. But
cross-method agreement only \emph{flags} disagreement; it cannot tell us which side is right. Only
comparison to a trusted standard can do that. We recommend reporting cross-method agreement routinely
while stressing that it does not substitute for a human anchor.

\paragraph{What this does and does not say about AI policymaking.} We are deliberately cautious. The
one robust, instrument-independent pattern is that Democrats engage with AI far more, and more
intensively, than Republicans. A useful \emph{negative} result is that the apparent partisan gap in
evidence \emph{quality} from the naive measure does not survive a better judge. We do \emph{not} claim
that ``most claims are supported'': that level ($\approx$0.5--0.7 under our judge) is conditional on an
unvalidated, possibly self-preferring judge, is inflated by relevance-only retrieval and by
bill-text/government ``references,'' and could move substantially under human coding. The contribution
is a template plus a cautionary tale, not a measurement of how well legislators' AI claims are backed
by science.

\paragraph{A template, honestly scoped.} The design---cheap open-source surrogate, a small
high-quality gold set, PPI/DSL, no API, no RA---is a useful scaffold that lowers cost and access
barriers, but its value is realized only when the gold is trustworthy; with an LLM gold it is a
feasibility scaffold awaiting validation. Reproducibility (one command, no key) is necessary but not
sufficient: perfectly reproducing an unvalidated measurement reproduces its bias.
